## About Me

[Al Mouayed Bellah Nafeh](https://www.linkedin.com/in/al-mouayed-bellah-nafeh/) (b. 1993) earned his B.Sc. in Civil and Environmental Engineering from the University of Balamand, Lebanon (2015), where he completed a thesis on finite element analysis of structural steel elements. He then gained early professional experience on infrastructure and high-rise residential projects in Lebanon.

In 2017, he graduated from the European Commission’s Erasmus Mundus [MEEES](https://www.preventionweb.net/resource/erasmus-master-earthquake-engineering-and/or-engineering-seismology-meees) programme with an M.Sc. in Earthquake Engineering and Engineering Seismology, following studies at Université Grenoble-Alpes (France) and IUSS Pavia (Italy). His master’s thesis focused on numerical homogenization techniques for concrete. He subsequently completed a two-year research fellowship at Università degli Studi di Pavia and IUSS Pavia, funded by [ReLuis](https://www.reluis.it/en/) and ITERATE [ITERATE](https://civil-protection-humanitarian-aid.ec.europa.eu/funding-evaluations/financing-civil-protection/prevention-and-preparedness-projects-civil-protection/overview-past-track-i-and-track-ii-projects/improved-tools-disaster-risk-mitigation-algeria-iterate_en).

In 2023, he was awarded a Ph.D. with distinction (cum laude) in Earthquake Engineering from IUSS Pavia. His doctoral research advanced risk- and loss-based methodologies for large-scale assessment of non-ductile infilled reinforced concrete buildings.

Currently, he serves as Physical Vulnerability Lead at the [Global Earthquake Model Foundation](https://www.globalquakemodel.org/gem-team/mouayed-nafeh), where he leads efforts to assess regional seismic vulnerability and support risk transfer strategies. He is the primary developer of the OpenQuake Vulnerability Modellers Toolkit and contributes to the development and refinement of GEM’s global vulnerability model.

His research focuses on the numerical modelling and seismic analysis of regional building typologies, probabilistic demand models for building portfolios, and the estimation of both monetary and human losses from earthquakes—bridging applications in academia, industry, and disaster risk reduction.
